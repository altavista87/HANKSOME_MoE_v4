Why HANK Models Matter

Traditional models treat everyone as the same "representative agent." HANK models show us the reality: economic shocks hit the poor and rich very differently.

Aspect RANK (Traditional) HANK (Heterogeneous) Why It Matters
Households One "representative" agent Thousands of actual households Real people have different jobs, savings, debt
Policy Effects Same for everyone Depends on income/wealth Rate hikes hurt the poor more than the rich
Transmission Simplified Through household balance sheets Shows who gets hurt, not just how much
Shock Impact Aggregate only Distributional Floods hit farmers differently than bankers

Future Research Interests

This project is the beginning of a longer journey. These are the areas I hope to explore next.

1. Ageing Nation

Malaysia is transitioning to an ageing society (projected 14% elderly by 2030). How do HANK models capture:

  • Intergenerational wealth transfer pressures
  • Healthcare cost burdens on working-age households
  • Retirement adequacy across income distributions
  • Fiscal sustainability of pension systems

2. Gig Economy

The informal sector already represents 32% of Malaysian employment. Key questions:

  • Income volatility effects on consumption smoothing
  • Lack of social protection during economic shocks
  • Platform economy vs traditional employment dynamics
  • Policy tools for precarious workers

3. Education & Healthcare

How can public investment in human capital close the inequality gap?

  • Education quality gradients across income levels
  • Healthcare access as human capital investment
  • Returns to education in heterogeneous agent settings
  • Universal healthcare as automatic stabilizer

4. AI Nation

Malaysia's aspiration to become an AI hub has distributional implications:

  • Automation risk across skill levels
  • AI augmentation vs AI replacement effects
  • Digital infrastructure divide (urban vs rural)
  • New tax bases for automated economies

Collaboration Welcome: If you're researching any of these areas, I'd love to connect on GitHub and potentially extend EngramHANK to address these questions.

What I Learned About Malaysia's Economy

As a Malaysian citizen, I wanted to understand how my own country's economy responds to shocks. This analysis of 20 major economic events (2008-2025) revealed surprising patterns.

Important Limitations

Please interpret these results appropriately. This is a learning project with known limitations:

  • Author background: I am not a trained economist. This is citizen science, not expert research.
  • Model simplifications: No firm heterogeneity, simplified income process, closed economy assumptions.
  • Data estimates: Some shock magnitudes estimated from public reports rather than microdata.
  • Not for policy: Results are illustrative and exploratory. Do not use for actual policy decisions without expert validation.
